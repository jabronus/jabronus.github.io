2024 in Review

December 24, 2024

Hi there! It’s been a bit.

If I felt like last spring/summer was busy with speaking prep for Config 2023, this one felt doubly so. Also I was holding off on posting because of (unsuccessfully) trying to redesign this place for the last 11 months. Anyway here are some things that happened since the last time I wrote in January:

Into the Awesomeverse

I helped launch and maintain our Kickstarter campaign for Web Awesome in the spring! I’d heard folks say that doing crowdfunding is way more work than you think it’s gonna be, and they weren’t kidding. Thankfully the Font/Web Awesome team is full of people much smarter than I am to help field questions and keep things compelling.

In addition I got to design lots of swag, some of which only just reached the US. Physical products are no joke! Shouts out to the fine folks at Cotton Bureau for handling fulfillment and helping get things across the finish line. Keep an eye out for extras in our CB shop once all the backer rewards go out.

In other Awesome news, we released all our remaining Sharp and Duotone styles, and brought Zach/11ty and Lea Verou on board. A big year — with even more big things coming in 2025... 🤫

Chunk

I wrapped up version 3 of my Chunk icon library! This was a big effort for li’l ol’ me — chipping away at an icon here and there after my kid went to sleep. Last year I’d pitched the improvements to Figma as part of their Creator Grant program and got accepted. Thankfully a concrete deadline for finishing the update wasn’t included in the contract, because it took me a few months longer than initially anticipated 😅 But along with completion came the chance to show off all my new pretties at Config 2024 — so I popped into Keynote of all things and whipped up a short promotional video:

Config

The Big Fig™ was a bit odd this year. Overcrowding, registration issues, and the overarching gloom of AI cruft cast a pall over the event. But the time, attention, sleepless nights, and love the Figma team put into every detail was also so readily apparent I really can’t fault anyone for the hiccups.

A huge highlight for me, though, was the people — a recurring theme for this year in general. I got to spend some top drawer quality time with my internet bud Alex eating burritos and perusing the Art of Noise exhibit at SFMoMA. I also got to show off our Font Awesome keycaps on a silver Typface Univers to anyone with a pulse and the slightest interest in computer input devices.

Issues aside, I’m planning on attending again in 2025 — if for no other reason than to get a burrito at Garaje and talk about video games and life goals with Alex again.

XOXO

It’s a weird time to be online.

That was a recurring theme at the very last XOXO festival — a place dedicated to the people who make things on the internet. And it was a theme I felt very keenly in a different way because it was my first and last time attending. I hadn’t even really been aware of XOXO until it had already been canceled; but looking back through festival videos and first-hand accounts, I knew I had to get there if Andy & Andy ever did it again.

By all accounts, this last hurrah was a stripped-back version of its former self: fewer days, fewer events, fewer musical guests. But that also meant it could be a more intimate and intentional space for those who’d come to call XOXO a second family.

As the kids say, the vibes were immaculate. (Do kids say that anymore?) Even having never attended an XOXO before, I felt integrated immediately — no doubt helped by the general air of inclusion around the whole event. A sensible COVID policy? In my 2024? It’s more likely than you think.

A few of my favorite talks: Cabel Sasser, Molly White, Craig Mod, Gita Jackson, Sarah Jeong, Kim Belair, Erin Kissane, and Dan Olson.

And some of the lovely folks I got to spend time with (including friends new and old): Ben Krogh, Arun Venkatesan, Christa Mrgan (sorry I didn’t hear your karaoke), Jez Burrows, Nathan Peretic, Lynn Fisher, Paul Straw, Paul Carroll, Susan Lin, and Taylor Moore.

I wish I could drink that pear shrub again.

Creative Works East

While stranded in the airport en route to Config (and missing out on a dinner at The French Laundry 🥲), I got a message from Josh Horton asking me if I wanted to run an icon workshop at the upcoming Creative Works East in Brooklyn. I’d always wanted to attend CW in the past but the timing never worked out with other travel commitments. So I said yes!

I’ve worked up lots of different icon education materials over the years in print and video form, but I’d never gotten to do a long, in-person workshop like this before. Not to mention those were free — so the added pressure of making it a good value for the people paying to hear me talk about small pictures was quite daunting.

Unfortunately working on my presentation and materials every day for 3.5 months — overthinking and over-designing every single aspect — really stressed me out. I’m an absolute weirdo whose brain thinks that its first attempt at something new should be perfect. And giving myself a little slice of each day to worry about perfection and “giving people their money’s worth” really put my psyche through the wringer.

But overall, it went well. I only had 4 registrants (conference attendance as a whole was low), but they were super engaged and asked fantastic questions. Having never run a 3-hour affair in the past, I ended up mistiming the pacing and had to rush through some bits to pack in as much info as I could. After a couple years of multiple speaking gigs, I’d like to lay low for a bit. But I would love to rework some of the Creative Works material into something more streamlined and useful in the future.

Again, this one really ended up being about the people. Getting to spend more time with my buds Daniel Seong, Julia Steffen, Rogie King, and Helena Zhang; and getting to meet internet friends Lisa Champ, Jeff Sheldon, and Kelli Anderson for the first time.

There’s more that could be said about each of these (and maybe I will), but I need to warm back up to this whole blogging thing. If there’s anything particular you’re curious about, let me know below with an electronic mail! ✌🏻
